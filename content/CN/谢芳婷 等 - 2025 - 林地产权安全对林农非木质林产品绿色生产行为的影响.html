4.3 对策建议基于上述研究结论，为促进林农绿色生产行为和林业绿色可持续发展，提出如下建议：一是完善林地产权制度的相关法律法规，从法律、事实和感知多维度加强林地产权安全性。首先需要进一步完善“三权分置”改革，未来还需进一步按照落实所有权、稳定承包权、放活经营权的总体要求，在原有基础上，进一步明晰土地类型、林地权属主体、林地权属界限，解决历史遗留问题。其次，实行“林地经营权证”制度，允许流转期限五年以上的林地经营权向不动产登记机构申请登记发证。证书可以用作林权抵押贷款、申报林业项目及其他有关行政管理事务的凭证。再次，建立智能化林权登记系统，引入区块链技术构建林权电子登记平台，开发移动端APP 实现林权变更实时备案，设立县—乡两级林权仲裁庭，配置专业调解员队伍，开发林权纠纷在线调解平台。最后，强化宣传引导，促进林农对林权的认识和理解。相关部门要完善林地产权制度的实施机制、保证政策的执行效果，正确地引导林农的认知，把深化集体林权制度改革政策向林农宣传好、解释好，将政策效果有效地转化为林农的主观认知，充分调动林农绿色生产行为。二是结合引导性政策和约束性政策，提高林农对绿色生产技术的认知。首先，在引导性政策方面，地方政府应结合林农需求，开展林业技术培训，丰富培训方式，提高技术培训的覆盖范围和普及力度，加强相关政策宣传，丰富拓宽绿色生产技术的宣传渠道。同时，考虑林农差异性特征，鼓励林业大户及村两委带头践行林业绿色生产技术，利用农村熟人社会网络加强绿色生产互动，扩大同群效应对林农采纳绿色生产行为的影响。其次，在约束性政策方面，优化规则设计，将惩罚机制与正向激励（如补贴、技术援助）结合；增强林农参与性，确保村规民约由村民协商制定，提升规则认同感；开展分层治

Abstract: The security of forest land property rights constitutes the institutional foundation for

sustainable production practices among forest farmers, while their green production behaviors are a direct manifestation of such practices. This research examined the mechanisms through which different dimensions of land property rights security—legal, factual, and perceptual—af‐ fect the green production behavior of forest farmers, to provide insights for advancing the re‐ form of the collective forest rights system. Grounded in theories of property rights and house‐ hold behavior, this study employed Logit and Ologit models, utilizing 2023 survey data from 655 households in the collective forest areas of Zhejiang and Jiangxi provinces. The research investigated the impact of these dimensions of forest land property security on the probabilities and the extent of green production technology adoption by forest farmers. It further assessed the moderating effects of forestry technical training and village regulations. The findings indi‐ cated that: (1) 69% of forest farmers engaged in green production practices, with an average of 0.88 green forestry technologies adopted. (2) The legal, factual, and perceptual dimensions of forest property rights security had a significant positive impact on the probability and extent of forest farmers' adoption of green production technology. (3) Forestry technical training and vil‐ lage regulations significantly and positively moderated the impact of legal, factual, and percep‐ tual property rights security on the likelihood of green production technology adoption by for‐ est farmers. Finally, the study revealed that the impact of property security was not uniform across forest farmers, with those having higher forestry income and smaller land holdings being more sensitive to the effects of land property security. In conclusion, to promote the transforma‐ tion and modernization of forestry production, it was essential to improve the forest land prop‐ erty rights system while enhancing green production training and strengthing village regula‐ tions. These coordinated efforts would foster a conducive environment for green development
